Chris Fang, 方威凱

Managing Partner, Amaris Partners

Christopher Fang is the founder and CEO of Amaris Partners, a company based in Boston and Asia that focuses on developing life changing medical technology. One of its key mandates is to establish a global digital health foundry by bringing together the brightest minds across disciplines.

Previously a Managing Board Member and Worldwide Vice President at Johnson & Johnson Orthopedics, Christopher’s career began at Idenix Pharmaceuticals where he led two global registration trials for Hepatitis B. He was later appointed as Medical Director at Mitsubishi Pharma before moving on to Medtronic Vascular. As the Head of Clinical Affairs, Christopher was responsible for coronary and peripheral vascular development and corporate ventures.

Christopher earned his MBA from the Wharton School, his MD from Cornell University Medical College, and his Masters from Johns Hopkins School of Public Health. He was trained in general surgery at University of Massachusetts Medical School and Medical Informatics at Harvard-MIT Health Sciences and Technology program.

Christopher was awarded the Eisenhower Fellowship in 2011. He is an Advisor for Global Health Corps and Harvard SEED Social Innovation Program. He recently founded NTU LIVE, an executive training program that prepares future CEOs to lead biomedical startups.
